Despite the tremendous support these foreign workers has given us, Hong Kong is lacking behind in protecting their rights.

A Hong Kong NGO, Justice Centre, has interviewed over 1,000 foreign domestic workers in 2016.

39% of respondents said they did not have their own bedroom, and over 35% shared a room with a child or an elderly individual.

Besides, 2 in 3 workers have been working over 12 hours a day, way more than the globel benchmark, 8 hours for a 48-hour work week

Taking the Minimum Allowable Wage for Foreign Domestic Workers, HK$4,110 (US$526.9) per month at the time the survey was conducted, this would translate to a salary of HK$14.39 (USD $1.85) an hour, just equal to 44.3% of the minimum wage for local workers in Hong Kong.

The Statutory Minimum Wage (for local workers only) is currently HK$32.50, although workers who earn this amount have to pay for their own food and accommodation from their earnings).
